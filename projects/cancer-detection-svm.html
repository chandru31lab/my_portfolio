An improved cancer detection methodology leveraging SVM models with enhanced accuracy, published in IEEE Xplore. Demonstrates novel feature engineering and classification techniques.

📋 Research Overview

This research paper presents an improved approach to cancer detection using Support Vector Machine (SVM) classification models. By applying advanced feature selection techniques and optimized kernel functions, the proposed method achieves higher accuracy compared to existing approaches.

The study was conducted on publicly available cancer datasets and the results were benchmarked against state-of-the-art methods. The paper was accepted and published in IEEE Xplore, contributing to the growing body of research in medical AI.

⚡ Key Contributions

  • Enhanced Feature Selection — Novel feature engineering pipeline that identifies the most discriminative biomarkers
  • Optimized SVM Kernels — Comparative analysis of RBF, polynomial, and linear kernels with hyperparameter tuning
  • Improved Accuracy — Achieved higher classification accuracy over baseline methods
  • Cross-Validation — Rigorous k-fold cross-validation to ensure model generalizability
  • Reproducible Results — Complete methodology documented for research reproducibility

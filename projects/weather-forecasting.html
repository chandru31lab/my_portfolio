Deep Learning for
Weather Forecasting

Research on applying deep learning architectures for improved weather prediction accuracy, published in Springer. Demonstrates novel neural network approaches for meteorological data analysis.

📋 Research Overview

This research explores the application of deep learning techniques to weather forecasting, addressing the limitations of traditional statistical and numerical weather prediction models. By leveraging neural network architectures, the proposed approach captures complex non-linear patterns in meteorological data for improved prediction accuracy.

The paper presents a comparative analysis of different deep learning models including LSTM, CNN, and hybrid architectures, demonstrating their effectiveness in predicting temperature, precipitation, and other weather parameters. Published in Springer.

⚡ Key Contributions

  • Novel DL Architecture — Custom neural network topology designed for time-series meteorological data
  • Multi-Parameter Prediction — Simultaneous forecasting of temperature, humidity, wind speed, and precipitation
  • LSTM Networks — Long Short-Term Memory networks for capturing temporal dependencies in weather patterns
  • Data Preprocessing — Advanced normalization and augmentation techniques for weather data
  • Benchmark Comparison — Performance evaluation against traditional forecasting methods
  • Accuracy Improvements — Demonstrated significant RMSE reduction over baseline models
